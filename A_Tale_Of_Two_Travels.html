A Tale of Two Travels


This map was my final project for my Introduction to Cartography class. The map is meant to show the contrast between an Indigenous and Spanish perspective of geography in Ecuador by mapping the text from two books: "Kuntur Kuyashkamanta", a children's book of an ancient Kichwa legend, and "Descripción Geográfica de la Real Audiencia de Quito", an account of a Spanish traveller in Ecuador in the 1700s. Kichwa is the largest of Ecuador’s 13 Indigenous nationalities and the language is spoken by over 7 million people in South America, though it is not well-known in the United States. I have taken both Kichwa and Spanish at UW-Madison, so I wanted to make a map that combines what I've learned in these classes. With this map, I hope to provide a little introduction to those unfamiliar with the Kichwa language and culture of Ecuador. As I am not Indigenous myself, I only represented the places and paths explicitly mentioned in the books.
